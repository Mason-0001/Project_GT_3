About Tornadoes

Tornadoes are deadly natural disasters with extreme winds that can exceed 200 miles per hour. Every year, more than 1,200 tornadoes touch down in the United States alone. Tornadoes develop rapidly, giving little to no warning of when or where they will strike. We have developed these visualizations to help people better understand the dangers of tornados in their area or any area they are going to.

Tornado Data source

We are using data from the Storm Prediction Center of the NOAA’s National Weather Service which has the best data available on tornados in the U.S. Our data shows all tornadoes that registered on the EF Scale in the United States from 2007 to 2021. This data was compiled by the NOAA from a variety of sources which include but are not limited to: county, state, and federal emergency management officials, local law enforcement officials, skywarn spotters, NWS damage surveys, newspaper clipping services, the insurance industry, and the general public.
